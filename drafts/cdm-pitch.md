# CDM pitch (cdm.link)

**Venue:** [CDM — Create Digital Music](https://cdm.link/) · earned-media long shot · **Week 4 · 🎬 demo-gated**

### Before you send
- This is a **long shot** — CDM gets a lot of pitches. The bar is "genuinely interesting to a music-tech audience," not "please cover my project."
- **Do not send without the demo.** The hook is seeing/hearing AI dial in a patch on real hardware.
- Keep it short. One angle, one link, one demo. No press-release voice, no attachments.
- Send from a real email; find the current submissions/contact route on cdm.link (don't guess an address).
- It's fine if you never hear back. One polite follow-up after ~1–2 weeks is the limit.

### Subject
```
AI recreates a song's synth patch on real hardware (open source)
```

### Paste-ready email body
```
Hi [name if known, otherwise "CDM team"],

I built an open-source tool that listens to a song, works out how its synth/keyboard part was
made, and recreates that patch on a real hardware synth over MIDI — it sets the parameters
itself rather than loading a preset. It runs on gear your readers actually own (Nord Electro
5D, Roland JUNO-X, Prophet-6), and there's an on-screen mock synth so anyone can try it
without hardware.

Short demo (recreating the patch from [SONG] on a [synth]): [DEMO]

Why it might interest CDM specifically: it's a concrete, hands-on take on "AI + hardware
synthesis" — not a cloud plugin, not vaporware. The hard, still-unsolved part (mapping a
measured sound to specific ADSR/modulation/oscillator settings) is being developed in the
open, which I thought might make for an honest angle on where this tech actually is today.

Everything's free and open source (GPL-3.0): https://github.com/uribrecher/sounds-and-recreation

Happy to answer anything or do a quick walkthrough.

Thanks for your time,
[Your name]
[link to your site / the project]
```

### After you send
- One polite follow-up after 1–2 weeks max. Then let it go — it's earned media, not owed.
